1. What are the major classical dance forms of India?
India has several classical dance forms, each with its unique style, music, and cultural significance. The major ones are:
  • Bharatanatyam (Tamil Nadu)
  • Kathak (Uttar Pradesh)
  • Kathakali (Kerala)
  • Kuchipudi (Andhra Pradesh)
  • Manipuri (Manipur)
  • Mohiniyattam (Kerala)
  • Odissi (Odisha)
  • Sattriya (Assam)
2. What is the significance of mudras in Indian classical dance?
Mudras are symbolic hand gestures used in classical dance to convey emotions, stories, or themes. They play a crucial role in Bharatanatyam, Kathak, and other classical styles. Each mudra has its own meaning, such as representing nature, deities, or specific actions.

3. How is music involved in traditional Indian dance?
Music plays a vital role in Indian dance forms, providing rhythm and mood. Classical Indian dance is usually accompanied by live music, which can include traditional instruments like the tabla, mridangam, sitar, veena, flute, and vocals. The music often follows specific ragas (melodies) and talas (rhythmic cycles), which match the dancer’s movements and expressions.

4. What are some famous Indian dance festivals?
Some of the major dance festivals in India are:
  1. Khajuraho Dance Festival (Madhya Pradesh) – Celebrates classical dance forms in the backdrop of the ancient temples of Khajuraho.
  2. Natyanjali Dance Festival (Chidambaram, Tamil Nadu) – A tribute to Lord Nataraja, the god of dance.
  3. Sulaimani Festival (Kerala) – Celebrates Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and other dance forms.
  4. Konark Dance Festival (Odisha) – Focuses on Odissi and other classical dances, held in the historic Konark Temple.
  5. Mahabalipuram Dance Festival (Tamil Nadu) – A celebration of Bharatanatyam and other dance styles.
